# Monday Lessons Learned — Pipeline Docs

**Purpose:** Turn Sansar's real-world experiences into video lessons by extracting the **core investment principle**, not the surface story.

---

## Core Concept

When Sansar describes something that happened, we extract:

1. **The Surface Story** — what happened on paper (e.g. "I lost money on SMCI")
2. **The Real Lesson** — the underlying investment principle (e.g. "even a perfect thesis can fail for an unforeseen circumstance; diversification matters")
3. **The Video Hook** — a punchy, Sansar-style angle that makes people want to watch

**The surface story is never the video.** The lesson is the video.

---

## Workflow Overview

1. Trigger: user runs `/monday`
2. Sansar describes a story from the week (trade, market move, article, observation)
3. **Extract** — I identify the surface story and the real underlying lesson
4. **Refine** — dialogue until the core lesson is clear and video-ready
5. **Write** — full video script built around the core lesson
6. **Deliver** — script to Notion, email link to sansarkarki10@gmail.com

---

## Step-by-Step

### 1. Story Intake

Ask Sansar: *"What happened this week?"*
He might say:
- "I lost money on [ticker]"
- "[Stock] moved [X]% on [news] and I didn't expect that"
- "I read about [topic] and it made me think about [principle]"
- "I made a trade based on [thesis] and it worked/didn't work"

Keep listening until you have the full story.

---

### 2. Extract & Refine (dialogue loop)

For every story Sansar shares, map it through this filter:

**Surface Story** → what happened, the ticker, the outcome
**Real Lesson** → what is the *actual* principle an investor should take away?
**Video Hook** → how would Sansar title this as a YouTube video?

**Examples:**

| Surface Story | Real Lesson | Video Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Lost money on SMCI after fraud revelation | Even a perfect thesis can fail for an unforeseen black-swan event; diversification is not optional | "I Had a Perfect Thesis and Still Lost — Here's What I Learned" |
| NVDA rallied 15% in a week on AI news | High-conviction bets on trends you're early on can pay off massively — but being early requires patience | "Being Right but Too Early Almost Cost Me Everything" |
| Sold a stock right before earnings beat | Cutting a thesis short because of short-term anxiety means missing asymmetric upside | "I Sold Before the Rally — Don't Be Me" |
| Bought the dip on a bank stock that kept falling | "Catching a falling knife" — not every dip is a buying opportunity; thesis clarity must come first | "I Caught the Dip and It Kept Falling" |

**The consistency check:**
- Does this lesson hold for any investor, not just Sansar?
- Is it specific enough to be actionable?
- Is the video hook punchy enough to get a click?

If the story doesn't have a clear lesson (e.g. random noise, nothing learned), say so and ask for another.

---

### 3. Script

Once the core lesson is locked, write the video script:

- **Title** — YouTube-ready, punchy, Sansar's voice
- **[SECTION: X] labels** throughout (no numbers)
- **Hook** — lead with the story, name specific details
- **Pivot** — "Here's what actually happened, and here's what I learned"
- **Lesson** — state the principle clearly and early
- **Examples** — back it up with 1-2 real-world parallels or data points
- **TLDR** — color and opinion, not a bland summary
- **Standard disclaimer**

**Voice:** Chill delivery, spicy opinions. Conversational. Short punchy paragraphs. No walls of text.

**Delivery:**
- Put script in Notion first
- Email ONLY the Notion link to `sansarkarki10@gmail.com`
- Subject: `📹 [Date] — Monday Script`
- Never paste full script in email body

---

## Monday Dialogue Rules

1. **Always dig for the real lesson** — if Sansar says "I lost money on SMCI," the answer is NOT "SMCI is a fraud." The answer is about thesis risk, diversification, or black-swan events.
2. **Push back if the lesson is too surface-level** — "don't invest in frauds" is not a video. "Even good theses can die on binary events" is.
3. **Keep going until the lesson is crisp** — this is the hard part. Don't rush it.
4. **Be honest if nothing happened** — if Sansar can't think of anything, it's okay to say "sounds like a quiet week, let's skip Monday this time."
