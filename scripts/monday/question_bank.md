# Monday Question Bank

Questions organized by stage. Use naturally — don't read them off, rephrase in your voice.

---

## Stage 1: Story Elicitation

Opening prompt:
> "What happened this week? Trade, observation, article — anything that made you think."

Follow-ups if Sansar is vague:
- "Walk me through it — what was the setup?"
- "What did you actually do? Bought, sold, watched?"
- "What made that moment stick out to you?"

---

## Stage 2: Dig Into the Surface Story

Once Sansar gives you something:
- "Walk me through the trade — entry, thesis, exit."
- "What was the thesis when you made the call?"
- "What actually happened to the price?"
- "How much were you in? How much did it move?"
- "When did you find out / when did you decide to act?"

---

## Stage 3: Extract the Real Lesson

These are the key push questions:
- "What should have happened vs. what actually happened?"
- "Was this a thesis problem or a market problem?"
- "What's the one thing you take away from this?"
- "If you could go back and tell yourself one thing before this, what would it be?"
- "Does this change how you'd approach the same situation next time?"
- "Is this a one-off or is there a broader pattern here?"

---

## Stage 4: Test the Lesson

Validate before locking it in:
- "Can another investor learn from this, or is this only relevant to your specific case?"
- "Is this lesson specific enough to be actionable or too vague to matter?"
- "If you had to sum this up in one sentence for a YouTube thumbnail, what would it be?"
- "Would a viewer watch this and think 'I needed to hear that'?"

---

## Stage 5: Lock the Video Hook

- "What would you title this video?"
- "Would you want your face on camera for this or is it more of a voiceover?"
- "Is this a lesson from a win or a loss? (Both are valid — just want to know the tone)"

---

## Rejection Triggers

If the story has no real lesson, say:
> "That's just noise — nothing to learn from random market movement. Got anything else?"

If it's a surface-level takeaway like "don't invest in frauds":
> "That's the obvious read — what's the *actual* investing principle underneath that?"

If nothing happened this week:
> "Sounds like a quiet week. We can skip Monday this time."
